You may be wondering -- why should i care about open science and reproducibility. 1. Open science refers making your workflows open so that someone else can reproduce your work. This doesn't benefit the community of science of a whole but also benefits you. Imagine having to redo plots 6 months down the road after getting a journal review back. If your plots are easy to reproduce and your methods well documented... the time involved in redoing the effort will be less! --- class: center, inverse ## Open science & reproducibility * **Push science forward:** Others can push forward the work that you're doing. You can push forward the work others are doing. ??? By documenting your work, the next person who picks it up doesn't have to start over. Also in a peer review process your methods are completely transparent. This helps science as a whole move forward --- class: center, middle, inverse ## Opportunities for cool science There are SO many sources of data available to use! --- class: middle, center ## NASA EOS data have increase 122 fold in 1 decade .smaller-image[![ ]({{ site.url }}/images/slide-shows/earth-analytics-course/fall-2017/nasa-logo.png)] --- class: center, middle, inverse ## Remote sensing | collected from space ![ ]({{ site.url }}/images/slide-shows/earth-analytics-course/wk1-intro-earth-analytics/image16.png) --- class: center, middle, inverse ## Remote sensing | collected from airplanes (and helicopters, drones, etc.) ![ ]({{ site.url }}/images/slide-shows/earth-analytics-course/wk1-intro-earth-analytics/image17.png) ???
